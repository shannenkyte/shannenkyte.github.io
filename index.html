A little about me

MSc Student in Clinical Neuroscience & Brain Health at University of Toronto · Researcher at the Centre for Addiction and Mental Health · Crisis Counsellor · Education outreach · Mentor · Photographer

I'm a Master's of Science Student in Clinical Neuroscience and Brain Health at the Institute of Medical Science, Temerty Faculty of Medicine, University of Toronto. I hold a deep commitment to improving lives through research and clinical work, impacting lives through teaching and mentorship, and experiencing life through exciting escapades and my camera lens.

At the Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH) in Toronto, under the supervision of Dr. Philip Gerretsen and Dr. Victor Tang, I work with patients diagnosed with substance use and schizophrenia disorders using various research modalities such as brain imaging, longitudinal analyses, and brain stimulation interventions.

Aside from research, I am deeply driven by any kind of education — scientific and non-scientific — that excites you and changes how you see the world. I want people to be as excited about learning as I am, and I am committed to finding ways to do that through making learning fun and engaging. One day, I want to be a professor that students look up to and remember for years (and write amazing RateMyProf reviews about too of course! ><). For now, I'm building toward it: through my education channel on Instagram (@thekyteeffect), mentorship, and the MedComics Con.

Having grown up across countries with drastically different cultures — including India, Qatar, Japan, and Canada — I have gained a global perspective which allows me to appreciate differences in people and informs every aspect of my life. Outside of academia, I'm a huge FC Barcelona fan (Visca Barça, Visca Catalunya!), and I love spending time with friends and family, being in nature, working out, playing sports or board games, and trying out new food.

01 — Research & Publications

Knowledge is the beginning of Wisdom

My research at CAMH under Dr. Gerretsen and Dr. Tang investigates why some people lack awareness of their own mental illness — a phenomenon called anosognosia or impaired insight. Using fMRI, neuropsychological assessments, tDCS, rTMS, and caloric vestibular stimulation, I study impaired illness awareness in substance use disorders and schizophrenia to understand the neural correlates and improve patients' treatment trajectories.

02 — Clinical Work

Helping people in their most vulnerable moments

I believe that rigorous science should be in service of people, not just doling out research publications. Direct clinical and crisis work is how I stay accountable to that value — it's a reminder that behind every data point is a person, and that the most meaningful research and patient recovery occurs by working with individuals in their most vulnerable moments.

Passing it along

Mentorship is one of the most important aspects of my life. Professors and mentors throughout my life have shaped me into the individual I am today and have ingrained the values I hold dear. I owe a great debt of gratitude to these individuals. In order to honour what my mentors built in me, I am committed to mentoring high school and undergraduate students myself. Whether you're navigating grad school applications, trying to land your first research position, just figuring out what you want to do career-wise, or simply need a listening ear — I'm here for a conversation.
